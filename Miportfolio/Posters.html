Showcase

Showcase is a campaign against human trafficking for sexual exploitation. Through these designs, I aim to make people reflect and raise awareness about this issue and the social injustice that continues to exist in today’s society.
Both posters communicate the message in an ironic and metaphorical way.

In the first one, we see a catalog similar to those used for furniture, clothing, or food. However, in this case, it features mannequins of women with names written underneath. Some of them have labels like “Your sister” or “Your daughter” in a different color, appealing to people's emotions and encouraging more empathy.

The second poster shows a shop window, once again with mannequins. This time, there's an awning that reads “Butcher Shop,” and the mannequins are marked with labels like livestock. This refers to how women are treated as pieces of meat, completely dehumanized. When women are sold, the only thing that matters is the profit.

Veranos de la Villa 2024

Veranos de la Villa is a festival held in the city of Madrid, offering a variety of activities for all audiences, with a lively atmosphere and live music. Each year, a different edition is created, including a new poster design proposal.
Last year, as a class assignment, we were asked to come up with an idea for what the 2024 poster could look like.

In my case, I chose to use beach umbrellas and ice creams—two very iconic symbols of summer. I also included the Picasso Tower, a well-known building in both Madrid and Spain. It had appeared in posters from past editions, and I liked the symbolism it added to the design.
The poster has a vintage aesthetic, with grainy textures and earthy tones. I also used a bold, decorative typeface rather than a modern one, which tend to be more minimal and neutral.

JotDown Magazine

I designed these three posters as part of a class assignment, based on a collection of three articles from JotDown magazine.

“The Paradigm for Identifying Tyranny” explains how a villain only becomes dangerous once they gain power—power that is often granted by the people themselves.
“Pirates in Science” discusses how scientific research is increasingly being sold to journals without proper verification, leading to the spread of misinformation.
“The Economic Brain” explores how human decision-making plays a key role in consumer behavior.

For all three posters, I used a collage technique, combining a solid-colored background with a representative image related to each article.
